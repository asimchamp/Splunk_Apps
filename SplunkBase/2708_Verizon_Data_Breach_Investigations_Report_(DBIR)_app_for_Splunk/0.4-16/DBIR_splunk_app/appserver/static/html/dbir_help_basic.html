It All Starts Here

Thank you for downloading and installing the DBIR App for Splunk. We really appreciate it. Seriously. But now what do you do? It's interesting to look at, but how can you use this app to help your company monitor what is happening to you? Especially what's happening right now.

We're glad you asked.

As you know (since you're here already), Splunk offers a wide range of options for querying your data and finding those patterns that give a bit of insight into your machine data. We've tried to do the same. We've created a series of dashboards using Splunk's Simple XML Dashboard standard, with the focus on simple. What does this mean for you? It means you can update anything in this app to better fit your company and your data. You have our permission. (Just make sure you keep reading to learn how to upgrade to new versions of the DBIR App in the future.)


Reports are only as good as the underlying data.


Understanding how the DBIR App queries data will help you decide how best to add your own data into Splunk. We can't explain to you everything that you can do with Splunk. Luckily, they have one or two great sites to help you become a Splunk expert yourself.


HOW TO GET FROM HERE TO THERE?

We'll go into more detail on the data page, but the best way to determine how to begin...is to start at the end. In other words, if you want to know how to make your data appear in the colorful charts and not so colorful tables take a look at the queries themselves.

Every Splunk result set or chart will have a pack of icons on the bottom left that only appear when you hover over them. The first icon (shown) will either say Open in Search or Open in Pivot. If you click that icon, a new window will open and show you the exact query that was used to populate the chart or table.

But what am I looking for? That's a good question. We'll do our best to answer that on some of the other help pages. For instance:

  • The add-on help page has a lot of information about event types and tags, including some add-ons we think are rather important and others that might just save you some time.
  • And the data help page will show you how to choose which index to use when importing data. For instance, email data should be imported into an index called email. We did that on purpose.

UPGRADING THE DBIR APP

So you downloaded the app and made a bunch of customizations and then we released a new version. What do you do now?

Here's the good news: we won't overwrite your work. But there's bad news, too: for every page you change, you won't see our improvements! Luckily, there's more good news. Take a look a our customization help page and we'll show you how to make sure you can keep your changes and include any improvements we've made. That's what we call win-win.


A NOTE ABOUT DRILLING DOWN

Splunk does a lot of stuff for us. That's why we like it. One of these is the drilldown. When you're looking at a chart or a table and you see something that catches your eye, all you have to do it click it to "drill down" and get more information. Usually, this will open up another search in a new window for you, passing the drilldown search as a parameter in the URL. Not to get too technical, but some of these drilldown searches can get pretty involved.

Unfortunately, some browsers have a max limit for URL length. Internet Explorer (IE) is one of them. It limits URLs to 2,084 characters. We don't believe in limits. So if you are using IE and you drilldown and the page never finishes loading, take a look at the URL because IE may have cut us off right in the middle. Like, literally, cut off the end of the URL. We don't want to separate you and your data, so thought we'd warn you, just in case.

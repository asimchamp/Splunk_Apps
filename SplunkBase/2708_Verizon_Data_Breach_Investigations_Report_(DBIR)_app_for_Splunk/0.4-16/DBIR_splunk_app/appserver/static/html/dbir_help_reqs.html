To Add-on or Not to Add-on

It turns out that when we all work together, we get things done faster. Thankfully, Splunk has an entire website built for the sole purpose of sharing apps. Within the DBIR App, there are some add-ons that are necessary and others that could help. Depending on how you've ingested your data, it may be convenient to use objects created in various add-ons or you may want to create the objects yourself to ensure we are pointing directly at your data. It's your choice.

If you're reading this page, you've already visited splunkbase at least once. And as you look around you'll see a wealth of interesting add-ons and apps. When putting together the reports in the DBIR App, we chose to leverage some of these add-ons where it made sense. After all, I'm not sure any of us can improve upon the shape of a wheel.


SPLUNK'S COMMON INFORMATION MODEL

One of the most important is the Splunk Common Information Model. We don't like to bandy about words like mandatory very often, but this add-on is a must. When you're monitoring your security situation, you could have data coming from a variety of disparate sources, sources that don't necessarily play well together. The Common Information Model (CIM) provides a way for this data to easily be aggregated into one query, one chart, or one report. (And they're pretty thorough with their documentation, too.)

WHERE CAN I VERIFY THESE OBJECTS?

Log in as an administrative user and click on the Settings menu option. Underneath the Knowledge column, you'll see all sorts of contructs.

Follow the links for event types or tags or any other type of object and use the search option to location exactly what you're looking for. You can create your own or expand items that already exist to include your data. Because, after all, inclusion is a good thing.
